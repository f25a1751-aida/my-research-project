The Agro-Product Dilemma

In Malaysian oil palm milling, harvesting the Tenera fruit bunch (FFB) at the absolute peak of mesocarp oil content is crucial. If harvested under-ripe (low oil density), mills suffer immense extraction losses. If harvested over-ripe, cellular membranes break down, letting endogenous lipase enzymes rapidly hydrolyze triglycerides into Free Fatty Acids (FFA), rendering the Crude Palm Oil (CPO) low quality and hard to refine.

The Goal: Use the first derivative \( f'(x) \) to locate the optimal harvest point—where oil accumulation velocity slows down near maximum capacity, just before FFA accumulation escalates.

The Mathematics of Growth

Using growth data published by the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB), we model the oil accumulation percentage \( f(x) \) during the weeks after anthesis (WAA) using a sigmoidal Logistic Growth Function:

Oil Content Function:
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
Accumulation Rate (Derivative):
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
\( x \): Weeks After Anthesis (WAA)
\( L = 78\% \): Theoretical Max Yield
\( k = 0.55 \): Growth Constant
\( x_0 = 17 \): Peak Inflexion (Weeks)
